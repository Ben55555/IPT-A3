Premiere Pro

 

Key Frames:

Key frames are used in animation/filmmaking to define start and end points. They are called key frames because their positioning in time is defined as a frame in the video. Since there are only two key frames over a span of a couple seconds, it doesnt create the illusion of movement. Therefore, remaining frames are filled in between them.

As you can see below there are multiple key frames that are instructing the camera to move to a certain position, change scale and even opacity.

Additional features include making the keyframe a bezier which smoothens out the transition since its based on a mathematical formula called the Bezier curve.

 

Transition:

In my video there is a camera whip transition. To create this effect, I had to make an adjustment layer that had the special effect property Directional Blur and Posterize. The directional blur can specify how stretch the blur is and the angle. For my video I had it an angle of 90 degrees because I wanted the camera to look like it was whip from left to right.

Posterize changes the saturation of the image, this was used to enhance the blur during the center of the transition and by using keyframes I was able to smoothly transition the posterization from min to max at the start and max to min at the end.

Introduction:

The intro to the dance video first starts as a black screen, the letters then fade in with the video playing in the background, the camera then zooms into the letters revealing the video playing in the background.

This effect was made using a track matte key and special effect 3D object. The 3D object was used to make the text fly towards the camera by decreasing the distance of the text from the screen. The track matte key allowed a video layer to become an alpha layer which allows other layers to be displayed on that layer confined to whatever boundaries, in this case it was the letters on track 3

Embedding a video link/Optimization:

To embed a video onto the website is actually quite simple. All it takes is one simple line of code which tells the website to search for this url (in this case it is a youtube video) and display/stream onto the website.

This also makes the video optimizable for whatever resolution. For example if you minimized your browser or are using a mobile device, the stream resolution will scale down with whatever device you are using. Therefore if the video had a resolution of 4k, if viewed on a phone it would scale it down to 720p for example.

Photoshop

 

Layers:

Using layers in photoshop helps protect certain aspects of the file. For example if you wanted to write on top of something else but dont want to damage the original layer. You can make a layer on top, this will make you write on top of the original piece while keeping it intact and unharmed.
